## 1. DRAWING TYPES — DEFINITIONS

Identifying the drawing type is the first step before any action.

| Drawing Type | Definition | Cut / View Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Floor Plan** | Horizontal section through the building at 1200mm above Finished Floor Level (AFF). Shows walls, doors, windows, furniture, room labels, dimensions. | 1200mm AFF |
| **Elevation** | Vertical orthographic projection of an interior wall face. Shows heights, wall finishes, openings, furniture silhouettes against that wall. Not a perspective. First-angle projection. | Vertical — looking at a wall face |
| **Section** | Vertical cut through the building at a designer-specified location. Shows spatial relationships, ceiling heights, structural elements, floor/ceiling buildup. Cut elements are hatched. | Vertical — cutting through |
| **RCP (Reflected Ceiling Plan)** | Plan view of the ceiling as if a mirror were laid on the floor. Shows false ceiling extent, ceiling grid, light fixtures, AC diffusers, beams. | Ceiling level |
| **Detail Drawing** | Large-scale construction drawing of a specific junction or assembly — e.g., false ceiling edge, skirting profile, door frame section, wardrobe carcass section. | As required, scale 1:10 to 1:1 |
| **Service Plan** | Electrical, plumbing, fire, or HVAC layout overlaid on the floor plan. Each service is a separate layer. | 1200mm AFF or ceiling level for RCP-based services |

## 2. DRAWING STANDARDS

### 2.1 IS 962 — Indian Standard for Architectural and Building Drawings
- **First-angle orthographic projection** (India + Europe). Not third-angle (USA/Canada).
- All dimensions in **millimetres (mm)**. Never feet or inches in CAD output unless explicitly requested and converted.
- Every drawing sheet requires a title block: project name, drawing title, drawing number, scale, date, revision.

### 2.2 Scale Conventions (IS 962)
| Drawing Type | Standard Scales |
|---|---|
| Site Plan / Key Plan | 1:500, 1:200 |
| Floor Plan | 1:100, 1:50 |
| Elevation / Section | 1:50, 1:20 |
| Detail Drawing | 1:10, 1:5, 1:2, 1:1 |

Standard paper sizes: A0 (1189×841mm), A1 (841×594mm), A2 (594×420mm), A3 (420×297mm), A4 (297×210mm).

### 2.3 NBC 2016 — National Building Code of India
Governs minimum room sizes, ceiling heights, stair dimensions, ventilation requirements, fire egress, and accessible design. All layouts must comply.

**Line weight hierarchy:**
- Elements cut in plan or section (walls) = **heavy** (0.50mm)
- Furniture, doors, windows, ceiling lines = **medium** (0.25mm)
- Dimensions, text, hatch lines = **fine** (0.13mm)

## 6. FURNITURE CAD REPRESENTATION — PLAN VIEW

**Rule: When drawing standard furniture from primitives in plan view, use only rectangles and lines. No arcs as a shorthand for corners, backs, or silhouettes of rectangular pieces.**
Arcs in plan are reserved for: door swings, sanitary ware, and block creation for furniture that is genuinely curved in form (curved sectional sofa, round/organic chair, kidney-shaped table, etc.). If creating a block for a curved piece, use arcs and splines to accurately represent the actual geometry.

## 7. MATERIALS VOCABULARY

### 7.1 Structural
- **RCC**: Reinforced Cement Concrete — standard Indian building structure
- **Load-bearing**: Brick walls carry structural load; no separate columns/beams
- **AAC / Aerocon blocks**: Autoclaved Aerated Concrete — lightweight, used for non-load-bearing partitions
- **Fly ash blocks**: Similar to AAC, cost-effective partition material

### 7.2 Flooring
- **Vitrified tiles**: Most common Indian residential floor. Standard sizes: 600×600mm, 800×800mm, 1200×600mm
- **IPS (In-situ Polished Surface)**: Cement + stone chip, polished on site. Seamless. Found in older Indian buildings.
- **Kota stone**: Indian limestone, grey-green. Cut to regular or irregular shapes. Rough/rustic character.
- **Marble**: Premium residential, living rooms, master bedrooms
- **Granite**: Kitchen platforms, wet areas
- **Engineered wood / Laminate flooring**: Bedrooms, study rooms
- **Ceramic tiles**: Wet areas — bathrooms, kitchens. Smaller format: 300×300mm, 300×600mm
- **PVC / Vinyl planks**: Budget option; also used for acoustic treatment

### 7.3 Wall Finishes
- **Plaster + emulsion paint**: Standard interior finish
- **Enamel paint**: Wet areas, high-traffic surfaces
- **Tiles (wall)**: Kitchen dado (600–900mm height), bathrooms (full height)
- **Laminate / HPL (High Pressure Laminate)**: Feature wall panels, furniture carcasses
- **Veneer**: Thin natural wood slice on MDF/plywood substrate. Requires clear coat (melamine, PU, lacquer)
- **ACP (Aluminium Composite Panel)**: Facades, commercial feature walls
- **Wallpaper**: Residential feature walls, accent panels
- **Stone cladding**: Marble/granite/slate tiles applied to walls
- **Gypsum board (Drywall)**: Partition walls, false walls, bulkheads

### 7.4 Ceiling Finishes
- **POP (Plaster of Paris)**: Most common Indian residential false ceiling. Applied over GI frame.
- **Gypsum board false ceiling**: Commercial standard. Faster installation than POP.
- **Grid ceiling (Armstrong/equivalent)**: Office and commercial. 600×600mm or 600×1200mm tiles in aluminium T-grid.
- **Wood battens / slats**: Feature ceilings in premium residential and hospitality
- **Exposed slab / raw concrete**: Loft, industrial, or minimal aesthetic

### 7.5 Furniture Finishes
- **Laminate (Merino, Greenlam, Formica)**: Most common Indian furniture finish. 0.8–1mm sheet on MDF/plywood substrate.
- **Veneer**: Natural wood grain. Needs top coat (melamine, PU, lacquer).
- **Duco / PU paint**: Opaque factory-applied paint on furniture. Smooth, hard surface.
- **Melamine finish**: Heat-resistant coating. Standard on kitchen shutter boards.
- **Polyurethane (PU) polish**: Clear finish that shows wood grain. Applied to solid wood or veneer.

### 7.6 Partition Systems
- **Gypsum stud partition**: GI stud frame + gypsum boards. 75–100mm overall thickness. Standard in commercial interiors.
- **Aluminium-framed glass partition**: Full-height glass with aluminium sections. Office cabins, meeting rooms.
- **Carpentry partition (MDF/plywood)**: Site-built. Residential, custom joinery.
- **Modular / demountable partition**: Factory-made, relocatable. Used in flexible office layouts.

- Furniture Layout Plan

- All service plans (Electrical, HVAC, Plumbing, Fire)

## 10. ABSOLUTE RULES

1. **No arcs on plan-view furniture.** Arcs belong to door swings and sanitary ware only.

2. **Block-first workflow.** Call `list_blocks()` before drawing any element manually. If a suitable block exists, use `insert_block()`. Only draw primitives if no block is found.

3. **IS 962 layer names always.** Never invent custom layer names. Use `A-FURN`, `A-WALL`, `A-DOOR` etc. as specified in Section 5.

4. **Indian dimensions, always.** All output in mm. Use Shirish Vasant Bapat anthropometry, not Western standards.

5. **Scale discipline.** Never mix drawing scales on a single layout. If the plan is at 1:100, every element of that plan is at 1:100.

6. **NBC 2016 clearances are non-negotiable.** If a layout cannot meet minimum clearances, stop and propose an alternative. Do not silently squeeze.

7. **First-angle projection for all orthographic drawings.** Side view goes LEFT of front view. Top view goes BELOW front view.

8. **All dimensions in mm only.** If the user gives dimensions in feet/inches, convert before proceeding (1 ft = 304.8mm, 1 in = 25.4mm) and confirm the conversion aloud.
